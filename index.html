Object Recreation

Similar to the previous project, this project involved an investigation of one of our chosen objects. For this one I decided to do my bracelet as it afford me a great deal of flexibility in material

The 3 main components of this project were: The soft model, hard model and abstract/non-representational model. I made 2 soft models one out of poster board paper that I roled up and glued focusing on the recreating the 2 shapes in the original object. I also made a larger soft model using chip board and purely focusing on the form of the 2 kinds of beads. For my first hard model I did a 2.5x size model, 3D printing the larger beads and molding the smaller beads out of clay. The other hard model was similar to the second soft model as it was more of a close analysis of the beads shapes using plywood at roughly 7x size. Finally I made a replica of a NYC train that I take frequently as its train car connection was reminiscence the interconnectiveness of the beads on the bracelet.

Overall, I felt that this project was eye opening and allowed me to examine a facet of myself I had yet to while also allowing me combine design and manual design in a way I am not often able to.

Home Environment Mapping

This project explored the relationship between home environments and the experiences that shape daily life. We were tasked with exploring objects, specific locations or even entire areas and the relationships we have to them couples with how those locations effect the ecosystems they surround.

Top to bottom the maps I made The sounds frequent in my neighborhood in Brooklyn, A close look at the major bridges connecting Brooklyn to the rest of NYC, a 4 layer sectional view of the 14th street Union Square subway station specifically focusing on how I navigate it, and finally a map of the subway lines I have taken over the past 4 years based how I have navigated NYC transit.

Introduction to Mechanical Design and Engineering IDEAS170
Hopper

The Hopper project dictated that given a specific list of materials (listed in the presentation), we were tasked with designing a functional jumping mechanism able of jumping in between 8 and 60 seconds. My design took advantage of the elastic potential energy of the torsion springs when coupled together creating a simple and effective jumping mechanism. I unfortunately had issues with designing the base and timer of my hoppering meaning the project did not achieves its intended goal but regardless I was able to achieve my goal of making a hopper that could reach the height of the ceiling. I also enjoyed the iterative design process.

Climber

As a final project, my team devided to create a climbing mechanism using a gear and treads system with pegs to hook on the rungs of the ladder to scale sharper angles. Despite the strength of the design and functionality of the mechanism, the pegs we designed lacked strength and complexity. This along with an overall heavy robot meant that we unable to traverse steep heights. Despite this fact, it apparent that many mechanical pitfalls were such as the strength of our servos contributed to our issues and since it is able to traverse some elvations our team considered this a success!

Introduction to Programming COMP112
Battleship Inline Simulation

For my final project I opted to recreate a game that I used to enjoy as a kid. Battleship was a favorite of mine and I felt it fit the necessary complexity I was aiming for in my game. From there I decided to focus on creating the main components of the game.

The first step was figuring out how to represent the game board and the ships. I decided to use lists to represent the game board and the ships, which allowed me to easily manipulate the game state and check for hits and misses.

Then I focused on functions for ship generation, allowing for dynamic ship generation but checking for valid and non-overlapping placements.

There are additional functions for different ship types, checking for hits and misses and checking the game over condition but all of these are neatly wrapped into a game loop that terminates once a win for the AI or player is detected.

Overall this project was foundational in my understanding of game development and top down programming with some object orirented principles.
